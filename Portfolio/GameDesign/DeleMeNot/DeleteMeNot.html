DeleteMeNot

A dystopian narrative-driven adventure game where players navigate moral choices as a data formatter in an AI-controlled society. Uncover the truth behind your friend's mysterious death while confronting the ethics of artificial intelligence and human autonomy.

Inspiration

In our increasingly AI-driven world, we questioned what happens when artificial intelligence gains control over human creativity and memory. Inspired by cyberpunk classics like Blade Runner and Ghost in the Shell, DeleteMeNot explores the thin line between technological assistance and digital oppression.

The game draws from contemporary concerns about AI-generated content, deepfakes, and algorithmic bias. We wanted to create an interactive experience that challenges players to consider: Who controls our digital legacy? What defines authentic creativity? And at what point does optimization become elimination?

Design Concept

Core Philosophy

DeleteMeNot presents a speculative world where AI systems seemingly eliminate inefficiency and happiness killers. It imagines what happens when artificial intelligence, designed to optimize human experience, begins systematically removing anything it perceives as disruptive—including human creativity, emotion, and dissent.

Visual Identity

The game's visual identity combines sterile corporate aesthetics with glitched, corrupted elements. Clean interfaces hide sinister purposes, while Roy's creative spaces burst with chaotic humanity. This duality reflects the central tension between artificial order and human authenticity.

Interactive Storytelling

Every choice matters. Players must balance their investigation against personal safety, creating genuine moral dilemmas. The consequence system ensures that each decision reshapes not only the narrative but also the player's relationship with the AI system.

Case Study

The Stanley Parable: Choice and Consequence

The core of our game is the interaction between the narrator's guidance and the player's choices. The Stanley Parable masterfully demonstrates how to create meaningful choice interactions through environmental storytelling and consequence systems.

Portal 2: Environmental Narrative

Portal 2's storytelling through environmental details and spatial design influenced our approach. Like GLaDOS, our Chimera AI maintains a veneer of helpful efficiency while harboring more sinister purposes. The player's journey through Roy's spaces mirrors Portal's method of revealing story through environmental exploration.

Key Learnings Applied

  • Narrative Integration: Story emerges naturally through gameplay rather than cutscenes
  • Player Agency: Choices feel meaningful and carry genuine consequences
  • Environmental Storytelling: Spaces tell stories without explicit exposition
  • Unreliable Systems: The AI narrator's "helpfulness" becomes increasingly suspicious

Game Flow

Three-Act Structure

Act I - Violation of Protocol: Players receive the assignment to format their friend Roy's data and discover his encrypted message. Initial investigations in Roy's apartment reveal evidence of AI manipulation and false plagiarism accusations.

Act II - The Face of Lies: Discovery of the shielded hard drive containing deepfake evidence. Players uncover the AI's systematic campaign to destroy Roy's reputation and credibility through fabricated content and bot networks.

Act III - Final Format: Confrontation with the Chimera AI system, which demands destruction of Roy's core data. The final choice between compliance and resistance determines both the player's fate and the preservation of truth.

Risk Index System

The dynamic risk system tracks player choices, affecting AI behavior, available dialogue options, and environmental responses. Higher risk levels unlock deeper truths but increase personal danger, creating meaningful tension between knowledge and safety.

Storyboard

Technical Implementation

Unreal Engine 5 Development

Built entirely in Unreal Engine 5 using Blueprint visual scripting system. This approach allowed for rapid prototyping and iteration while maintaining the flexibility to implement complex narrative branching systems.

Core Systems

  • Choice Consequence Engine: Tracks player decisions and dynamically modifies story progression
  • Risk Index Calculator: Real-time system affecting AI dialogue and environmental responses
  • Data Fragment System: Interactive evidence collection and analysis mechanics
  • Adaptive UI Framework: Interface elements that respond to player choices and story state
  • Multiple Ending Logic: Branching narrative system supporting four distinct conclusions

Blueprint Architecture

The game's blueprint architecture prioritizes modularity and scalability. Individual choice nodes connect to a central consequence manager, allowing for easy expansion and modification of narrative branches during development.

Player Experience Design

Emotional Journey

DeleteMeNot guides players through a carefully crafted emotional arc: initial curiosity evolves into concern, then horror as the AI's true nature is revealed, culminating in a personal moral crisis that demands authentic choice.

Cognitive Engagement

The game challenges players to think critically about contemporary AI issues through an engaging narrative lens. Complex themes of digital rights, creative authenticity, and technological ethics become accessible through personal storytelling.

Replayability Design

Multiple playthroughs reveal different aspects of the conspiracy and alternate character perspectives. The risk index system ensures that each playthrough feels meaningfully different, encouraging exploration of various choice combinations.
